When we launched Arise Dothan, I wrote about Isaiah 60:1 — arise, shine, for your light has come. That verse gave us our name. It also gave us our starting point, because before anything else, before community, before mission, there's this: God's glory is already rising, and our first move is to turn toward it. We call that Move Up. It's the first of three Kingdom Rhythms that shape our life together, and over the next few weeks I want to spend some time on each one — starting here, with worship.

## Before anything else, we gather to worship

Here's how we say it on our About page, and I mean every word of it: we gather to worship Jesus, encounter God's presence, and be formed by Scripture and the Holy Spirit. That's Move Up. Not a warm-up act before the "real" part of church. Not a box to check before we get to the practical stuff. It's the whole foundation. Everything else — the community we build, the mission we carry into Dothan — flows out of what happens when God's people turn their attention to Him.

## What worship actually looks like on a Thursday

If you've never been to one of our Thursday gatherings, here's the honest version: there's no stage, no fog machine, no performance. We meet upstairs at Mural City Coffee at 6:00 PM, and worship for us looks like open Scripture, unhurried prayer, and honest conversation about what God is doing in our lives. It's Spirit-filled without being showy. We believe the Holy Spirit is present and active among us — not because we manufacture an atmosphere, but because we've asked Him to lead and we're doing our best to get out of the way.

## Formed, not just informed

There's a difference between hearing about God and being formed by Him, and Move Up is where that formation happens. We open Scripture together not to collect information but to let it work on us — to let it shape how we see God, how we see ourselves, and how we see the people around us. That's slow work. It doesn't happen in a single gathering. It happens Thursday after Thursday, as a people who keep showing up and keep saying yes to whatever the Spirit is doing.

## Why this comes first

I want to be honest about why Move Up comes before Move In and Move Out. It's not that worship matters more than community or mission — Follow, Become, and Do all matter together. But you can't multiply disciples out of an empty well. A church that skips worship and jumps straight to serving will burn out. A church that worships without ever moving in or out will turn inward and go stale. We believe Jesus is building His kingdom now, in and through the Church, and we get to join Him as we Move Up in worship first — because that's where we remember whose kingdom this actually is.

## An invitation

If you've been putting off visiting, let worship be the low-pressure entry point. Come sit with us on a Thursday. Bring your coffee, bring your questions, bring whatever you're carrying. Nobody's going to put you on the spot or ask you to perform faith you don't have. You can just be there, open a Bible next to people who are as ordinary and as hungry for God as you are, and see what it's like to Move Up with us.

The glory of the Lord is rising over Dothan. I'd love for you to rise with us and worship at the center of it.

— Pastor Brian
