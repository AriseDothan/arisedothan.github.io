This is the first post in a short series on what I like to call Spirit Baptism, the whole life a person lives in the Holy Spirit. A lot of folks hear "baptism in the Spirit" and picture one dramatic moment, usually somebody else's. I want to widen the lens. Spirit Baptism is the promise of the Father, given by the Son, and it covers the entire Christian life, from the very first breath of faith to the last. It begins with something quiet and enormous: being born of the Spirit.

## You can't organize your way into new life

We could build this church on strategy and effort, and plenty of organizations run fine on those. But a person is not born again by trying harder. Jesus said it plainly to a religious leader who had tried harder than anyone:

> Flesh gives birth to flesh, but the Spirit gives birth to spirit. You should not be surprised at my saying, "You must be born again."

That's John 3:6-7. New life is not self-improvement with a cross on top. It is a birth, and you no more manufacture it than a baby arranges its own arrival. It is a gift of the Spirit.

## The Spirit given at the start

Here is the part people miss: you don't have to wait and hope the Spirit shows up someday. If you belong to Jesus, the Spirit has already come. After the resurrection, Jesus stood among his followers, breathed on them, and said, "Receive the Holy Spirit" (John 20:22). The Christian life doesn't start dry and empty, waiting for a spiritual upgrade. It starts with God himself taking up residence in you.

That means being born of the Spirit is not a second-class experience for beginners while the "real" Spirit-filled life belongs to a spiritual elite. Every believer, the newest and the shakiest included, is born of the Spirit. It is the front door, not the reward at the end.

## Welcomed into the life of God

When the Spirit gives you birth, he doesn't just hand you a fresh start. He folds you into the life of God himself, Father, Son, and Holy Spirit. You are given by the Father, through the Son, by the Spirit, into a family. That is a staggering thing to say about an ordinary person on an ordinary Thursday: God has made his home in you, and you in him.

## So, come and be born

If you have never begun, this is the beginning, and it is not complicated. Turn toward Jesus, trust him, and receive the Spirit he gives. You don't have to clean yourself up first or understand everything. Being born of the Spirit is how a life comes awake to God, and it is offered to you as you are.
