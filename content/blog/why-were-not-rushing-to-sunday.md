If you've followed Arise for any length of time, you've probably noticed something: we don't have a Sunday morning service yet. For some folks that's confusing — isn't that the whole point of a church? I want to be honest about why we're taking the road we're taking, because it's one of the most important decisions we've made.

## The temptation to sprint

The common way to plant a church is to sprint toward a launch date. Book a venue, build a band, print the flyers, get a crowd in the room on a Sunday, and call it a church. There's nothing evil about any of that — but it's easy to end up with a service that *looks* like a church without the thing that actually makes one.

We didn't want to rush to fill a room. We wanted to build something that lasts.

## Disciples first, then the crowd

So here's our order of operations, and it's on purpose: **disciples first.** Right now our core rhythm is the Thursday Disciple Making Community — a smaller group doing the slow, unhurried work of following Jesus together. That's where our launch team is being formed, one honest conversation at a time.

Why that order? Because a Sunday gathering is only ever as healthy as the people carrying it. Fill a room with strangers and you have an event. Fill it with disciples who've learned to follow Jesus, pray, open the Scriptures, and love each other in the ordinary weeks — and you have a church that can hold up under weight. We'd rather spend this season building people than building a production.

## Not a waiting room

I want to be clear: this isn't a holding pattern. We're not biding time until the "real" church starts. What happens on a Thursday *is* the real work of the church — making disciples who make disciples. Sunday mornings will come, and when they do, our Disciple Making Communities won't disappear; they'll remain and multiply, because that was always the engine, not a stepping stone we kick away once we launch.

Every dawn starts small. We're okay being small and real right now, because we're praying for something that outlasts a launch day.

## Room to build from the ground up

Here's the invitation hidden in all of this: there's room for you to help build this from the foundation. Most people who join a church walk into something already finished. Right now you could help *form* it — praying, gathering, serving, and shaping the kind of church Dothan will walk into on our first Sunday and for years after.

If that stirs something in you, I'd love to talk. Text Pastor Brian, come to a Thursday, and see what it looks like to build slow and build right.

— Pastor Brian
